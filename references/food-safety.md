# Food Safety Reference

Load this reference when checking or setting critical temperatures, especially for proteins.

## FDA/USDA Minimum Internal Temperatures

| Food | Minimum Internal Temp | Rest Time |
|------|----------------------|-----------|
| Poultry (all cuts) | 74°C (165°F) | None required |
| Ground meat (beef, pork, lamb) | 71°C (160°F) | None required |
| Beef steaks/roasts | 63°C (145°F) | 3 minutes |
| Pork (chops, roasts) | 63°C (145°F) | 3 minutes |
| Fish & shellfish | 63°C (145°F) | None required |
| Eggs | 71°C (160°F) | None required |
| Leftovers/reheating | 74°C (165°F) | None required |

## Time-Temperature Equivalents (Pasteurization)

Lower temperatures are safe if held for longer. This matters for sous vide and low-temperature cooking:

| Beef/Pork Internal Temp | Hold Time for Equivalent Safety |
|------------------------|-------------------------------|
| 54.4°C (130°F) | 112 minutes |
| 57.2°C (135°F) | 36 minutes |
| 60°C (140°F) | 12 minutes |
| 63°C (145°F) | Instant (USDA standard) |

## Danger Zone

- 4°C to 60°C (40°F to 140°F) is the bacterial growth danger zone
- Food should not remain in this range for more than 2 hours total
- When cooling leftovers, move from 60°C to 4°C within 2 hours

## Braising Context

Braising at 88-92°C liquid temperature for 90+ minutes far exceeds pasteurization requirements for beef. The safety concern in braising is not under-cooking — it's ensuring the liquid temperature stays above 74°C throughout. A properly maintained braise is inherently safe.

## When to Flag

Alert the cook if:
- A protocol sets a target below the USDA minimum for that protein without specifying time-temperature hold
- The cook reports temperatures significantly below target during a hold
- Cooling food is sitting in the danger zone approaching the 2-hour limit
